# Meridian Airlines — Boarding Procedures Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-CK-002
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All fare classes, all routes

---

## 1. Gate Arrival Times

Passengers must be at the departure gate no later than:

| Route Type | Gate Arrival Deadline |
|---|---|
| Domestic | 20 minutes before departure |
| International Short-Haul | 30 minutes before departure |
| International Long-Haul | 40 minutes before departure |

Meridian Airlines will not reopen the boarding gate after it has closed. Passengers who miss boarding are classified as no-shows (MA-CX-004).

---

## 2. Gate Close Times

| Route Type | Gate Closes Before Departure |
|---|---|
| Domestic | 15 minutes |
| International Short-Haul | 20 minutes |
| International Long-Haul | 25 minutes |

---

## 3. Boarding Zones

Meridian Airlines uses a zone-based boarding system to reduce congestion. Zones are printed on the boarding pass.

| Zone | Passengers | Boarding Order |
|---|---|---|
| Zone 1 | First class | 1st |
| Zone 2 | Business class | 2nd |
| Zone 3 | Meridian Miles Platinum members | 3rd |
| Zone 4 | Meridian Miles Gold members + passengers with infants + special assistance | 4th |
| Zone 5 | Meridian Miles Silver members + Economy Flex (window seats) | 5th |
| Zone 6 | Economy Flex (middle and aisle seats) + Economy Saver (window seats) | 6th |
| Zone 7 | Economy Saver (middle and aisle seats) | Last |

---

## 4. Priority Boarding

The following passengers board in Zone 1–4 regardless of fare class:
- Passengers requiring special assistance (wheelchair, mobility impairment)
- Unaccompanied minors (accompanied to gate by Meridian staff)
- Passengers travelling with infants (under 2 years)
- Active military personnel in uniform

Priority boarding can also be purchased as an add-on for USD 12–25 (see MA-AN-004).

---

## 5. Boarding Pass Requirements

The boarding pass must display:
- Passenger full name
- Flight number (e.g., MA-204, MA-011)
- Origin and destination airport codes
- Departure date and time
- Seat number (e.g., 14C, 32A)
- Boarding zone
- Barcode or QR code for gate scan

Digital boarding passes (Meridian app or phone wallet) are accepted at all Meridian-operated gates. Some airports require a printed boarding pass — this will be noted in the check-in confirmation email.

---

## 6. Document Check at the Gate

At international departure gates, Meridian gate agents perform a final document check:
- Passport validity confirmed
- Visa or entry document check (where applicable)
- Travel health documentation (where mandated by destination country)

Passengers who fail the document check at the gate are denied boarding and are subject to no-show penalties per MA-CX-004. Meridian Airlines accepts no liability for denied entry at the destination.

---

## 7. Seat Changes at the Gate

Seat changes at the gate are subject to availability. Economy Saver passengers may request a seat change for free at the gate if seats are available. Upgrades at the gate must be processed through the gate agent and are subject to upgrade fees (MA-FK-003).

---

## 8. Flight Number Reference

All Meridian Airlines-operated flights are identified by the prefix **MA-** followed by 2–4 digits, e.g.:
- MA-204 (Dubai DXB → London LHR)
- MA-011 (New York JFK → Dubai DXB)
- MA-87 (domestic short-haul)

Codeshare flights operated by partner airlines retain the partner airline's flight number prefix.
