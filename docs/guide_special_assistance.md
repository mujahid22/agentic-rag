# Meridian Airlines — Special Assistance Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-SS-001
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All passengers requiring mobility or physical assistance

---

## 1. Requesting Special Assistance

Special assistance must be requested **at least 48 hours before departure** to ensure appropriate arrangements are in place. Requests made within 48 hours will be accommodated on a best-effort basis.

**How to request:**
- Online: meridianair.com → Manage Booking → Enter PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92) → Add Special Services
- Contact Centre: +1-800-MERIDIAN option 3 (Special Assistance)
- Email: specialassistance@meridianair.com

A Special Assistance Reference is issued upon confirmation: **MA-SA-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-SA-XKP192).

---

## 2. Wheelchair Assistance Categories

Meridian Airlines uses IATA standard wheelchair codes. When requesting assistance, specify the appropriate category:

| Code | Description | Service Provided |
|---|---|---|
| WCHR | Can walk but requires wheelchair for long distances | Wheelchair from check-in to gate and at destination |
| WCHS | Cannot climb stairs but can walk short distances | Wheelchair from check-in to aircraft door; boarding lift if needed |
| WCHC | Completely immobile; requires full assistance | Wheelchair throughout; assistance onboard; carried to seat |
| WCBD | Dry cell battery wheelchair | Transported in hold; battery remains connected |
| WCBW | Wet cell battery wheelchair | Battery must be removed; handled per dangerous goods rules |
| WCMP | Manual power wheelchair | Transported in hold |

All wheelchair assistance is provided **free of charge** on all Meridian flights.

---

## 3. Boarding Assistance

Passengers with mobility impairment:
- Board in **Zone 4** (before general economy boarding) — see MA-CK-002
- If the aircraft requires stairs (no jet bridge), a boarding lift or aisle wheelchair is provided
- At least one aisle seat is reserved near the front of Economy for mobility-impaired passengers

---

## 4. Onboard Accommodations

| Accommodation | Availability |
|---|---|
| Aisle wheelchair (ACWC) | Available on all wide-body Meridian aircraft |
| Adjustable armrest aisle seats | All Meridian aircraft |
| Lavatory with grab rails | All Meridian wide-body aircraft |
| In-seat assistance by crew | Available on all flights |

Passengers who require crew assistance during the flight should notify cabin crew at boarding and press the call button when needed.

---

## 5. Hearing and Vision Impairment

| Impairment | Service |
|---|---|
| Deaf / hard of hearing | Written communication available; visual safety briefing card provided; crew briefed |
| Blind / visually impaired | Verbal safety briefing provided; guide dogs accepted in cabin (see Section 6) |
| DeafBlind | Contact specialassistance@meridianair.com for tailored pre-arrangement |

---

## 6. Guide Dogs and Assistance Animals

Registered guide dogs and trained assistance animals are accepted in the cabin on all Meridian Airlines flights **free of charge**.

Requirements:
- Documentation: current vaccination records, guide dog/assistance animal certification
- Advance notice: minimum **48 hours** before departure
- The animal must remain on the floor at the passenger's feet or in a carrier under the seat
- Animals must be trained and under control at all times

Emotional support animals are **not** accepted in the Meridian cabin. They may travel in the cargo hold under Meridian Cargo pet policy.

---

## 7. Stretcher Service

Passengers who are medically required to travel on a stretcher may do so with advance medical clearance:
- Medical Clearance Form (MEDIF) required — see MA-SS-003
- Stretcher service requires booking of multiple Economy seats (typically 3–4 seats blocked)
- Fee: USD 600–1,200 depending on route and seats required
- Medical escort recommended; Meridian can arrange one for an additional fee

Request: medicalservices@meridianair.com at least **5 days** before travel.
