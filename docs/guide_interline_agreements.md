# Meridian Airlines — Interline Agreements Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-CS-003
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** Passengers on Meridian Airlines itineraries involving interline partners

---

## 1. What is an Interline Agreement?

An interline agreement allows Meridian Airlines to issue tickets that include flights operated by other airlines on a single ticket, even when no codeshare arrangement exists. The passenger buys one ticket (with one Meridian PNR, e.g., MA-XKPL92) but travels on multiple carriers.

This is distinct from:
- **Codeshare:** Meridian markets a partner's flight under its own flight number (MA-)
- **Interline:** Meridian issues a combined ticket that includes another carrier's flight under that carrier's own flight number

## 3. Single-Ticket Interline Benefits

When your itinerary is on a **single Meridian ticket** (MA-XXXXXXXXXXXXX) including interline segments:

| Benefit | Available |
|---|---|
| Checked baggage through-tagged to final destination | Yes |
| Single check-in at the first departure point | Yes (where permitted by partner) |
| Meridian-issued boarding passes for all segments | Yes (where e-ticketing supported) |
| Protected connection (Meridian liable if delay causes missed connection) | Yes |
| Meridian Miles earning on Meridian-operated segments | Yes |
| Meridian Miles earning on interline partner segments | No (no earning on pure interline) |

---

## 4. Baggage on Interline Itineraries

The most important rule for interline travel:

- **Single Meridian ticket:** Meridian's baggage policy applies for the entire journey. Bags are through-tagged at the first check-in point.
- **Separate tickets (self-connect):** Each carrier's individual baggage policy applies. Bags must be collected and rechecked at the connecting airport.

Example: If booking MA-XKPL92 shows flight MA-204 (DXB→LHR, Meridian-operated) connecting to flight NA-101 (LHR→EDI, NordAir-operated) on the same ticket, Meridian's Business class baggage allowance applies throughout both sectors.

---

## 5. Disruption Handling on Interline Itineraries

If a Meridian-operated segment is delayed, causing a missed interline connection **on the same Meridian ticket:**
- Meridian is responsible for rebooking the passenger at no charge, including on the interline partner
- Accommodation and meal entitlements per MA-DC-001 apply
- Contact Meridian ground staff at the Meridian service desk, not the partner airline's desk

If the delay occurs on the **interline partner's segment:**
- The interline partner is responsible for rebooking
- Meridian will assist in coordinating but is not financially liable
- Contact the operating carrier's ground staff or service desk

---

## 6. Limitations

- **No Meridian Miles on interline segments** unless the partner has a separate bilateral miles agreement with Meridian (rare)
- **Seat selection** on interline partner segments: not available via Meridian systems; must be done directly with the operating carrier
- **Lounge access** at interline partner airports: not guaranteed unless the passenger has SkyCircle Alliance status (MA-CS-002)
- **Self-transfer itineraries** (separate tickets): Meridian accepts no liability for missed connections
