# Meridian Airlines — Transit Visa Rules Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-VI-002
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All passengers transiting through Meridian hub airports

---

## 1. Types of Transit

| Transit Type | Definition | Visa Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Airside transit (TWOV) | Remain in international/sterile zone only; do not clear immigration | Usually no visa required (nationality-dependent) |
| Landside transit | Clear immigration, access public areas, hotel, city | Transit or entry visa required in most cases |
| Same-day connection | Connect within the airport on a single PNR | TWOV rules typically apply |
| Overnight transit | Airport transit requiring an overnight stay | Entry/transit visa usually required |

---

## 2. Dubai (DXB) Transit Rules

Dubai is a major Meridian hub with high transit passenger volumes.

### 2.1 Airside (TWOV) at DXB
- Passengers remaining airside do **not** require a UAE visa for transit, regardless of nationality, provided they do not clear immigration.
- All Meridian connecting flights at DXB are within the same terminal complex (Terminal 3) — airside transit is standard.

### 2.2 Landside Transit at DXB
- Passengers wishing to leave the airport (hotel, sightseeing, or overnight stay) require a UAE transit visa or entry visa.
- Visa on arrival is available for many nationalities at DXB for up to **96 hours**.
- UAE visa-on-arrival nationalities: EU passport holders, US, UK, Australia, Canada, Japan, and 50+ others. Check icp.gov.ae for current eligibility.
- Passengers requiring a UAE transit visa must apply in advance via the UAE GDRFA portal.

### 2.3 DXB Overnight Connections
If a Meridian booking (PNR e.g., MA-XKPL92) has an overnight connection at DXB:
- Meridian provides hotel accommodation at a partner hotel for **Business and First class** passengers (and Gold/Platinum members on any fare) at no charge for connections over 8 hours
- Economy passengers: transit hotel at discounted Meridian rate. Book via meridianair.com/transit-hotel or +1-800-MERIDIAN

## 3. London (LHR) Transit Rules

### 3.1 UK Visitor in Transit (DATV)
A **Direct Airside Transit Visa (DATV)** is required for certain nationalities transiting through LHR without a UK visitor visa. Nationalities requiring DATV include nationals of Afghanistan, Albania, Bangladesh, and others — full list: gov.uk/transit-visa.

### 3.2 Landside Transit at LHR
UK Standard Visitor Visa or UK ETA (Electronic Travel Authorisation) required for nationalities who require it to enter the UK. UK ETA is required from November 2025 for all visa-waiver nationals not already holding a BRP or UK visa.

### 3.3 Connecting Across Terminals at LHR
Meridian operates from Terminal 4 (T4). Connections to other airlines at different terminals require clearing UK Border Force, which requires appropriate immigration permission.

## 4. New York (JFK) Transit Rules

The United States does **not** have an airside transit arrangement. All passengers arriving at JFK, including those connecting to onward flights, must:
- Clear US Customs and Border Protection (CBP)
- Hold a valid US visa or ESTA (Visa Waiver Programme)
- Collect and recheck baggage

Passengers transiting through JFK must hold the same documentation as passengers entering the US. There are no exceptions. Apply for ESTA at esta.cbp.dhs.gov at least 72 hours before travel.

## 5. Transit Hotel Booking References

For Meridian-arranged transit hotel accommodation at DXB:
- Booking reference format: **MA-TH-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-TH-XKP192)
- Available for connections of 6 hours or more
- Hotel shuttle arranged; Meridian ground staff coordinate with the hotel
- Request at the Meridian Transfer Desk at DXB arrivals or in advance by contacting transit@meridianair.com
