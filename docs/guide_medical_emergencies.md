# Meridian Airlines — Onboard Medical Emergency Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-HS-002
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All Meridian Airlines flights

---

## 1. Meridian Medical Capability

All Meridian Airlines flights are equipped with:

| Equipment | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| First Aid Kit | All flights | Bandages, antiseptics, basic medications |
| Enhanced First Aid Kit | All international flights | Additional medications, blood glucose meter |
| Emergency Medical Kit (Doctor's Kit) | All flights | Prescription medications, IV supplies, AED leads |
| Automated External Defibrillator (AED) | All flights | Cabin crew trained in AED use |
| Oxygen cylinders | All flights | Fixed and portable units |
| Stretcher | Wide-body international flights | Foldable stretcher in overhead storage |
| Sat-med ground support | All flights | Real-time radio/satellite link to ground physician |

---

## 2. Cabin Crew Medical Training

All Meridian cabin crew hold:
- Valid First Aid and CPR certification (renewed annually)
- AED operation certification
- Training in recognition of common medical emergencies (cardiac arrest, stroke, anaphylaxis, diabetic emergency, seizure, childbirth)

Senior cabin crew (Purser / Cabin Manager) additionally hold:
- Advanced First Aid certification
- In-flight medical emergency management training

---

## 3. What Happens During a Medical Emergency

### Step 1: Crew Assessment
Cabin crew will assess the passenger and administer first aid per their training. They will attempt to identify the nature of the medical emergency.

### Step 2: Medical Announcement
If the situation requires medical expertise, the Purser will make a PA announcement requesting any medical professionals (doctors, nurses, paramedics) onboard to identify themselves. Meridian crew coordinate with any volunteer medical professional.

### Step 3: Ground Medical Consultation
The flight deck contacts **MediLink** (Meridian's contracted ground-based medical advisory service) via satellite communications. MediLink provides real-time guidance to crew and assesses the severity of the situation.

### Step 4: Diversion Decision
The captain, in consultation with MediLink and crew, decides whether to:
- Continue to destination
- Divert to the nearest suitable airport
- Declare a medical emergency and request priority landing

The captain's decision is final and is made solely on safety grounds. Diversion may occur even when the passenger believes they can continue.

---

## 4. Diversion Policy

If an aircraft diverts for a medical emergency:
- All passengers are affected by the diversion
- Meridian will rebook all affected passengers on the next available flights at no charge
- Hotel accommodation provided at Meridian's expense if diversion requires an overnight stay
- The diverted passenger's onward travel is arranged at no charge regardless of fare class
- No cancellation fees apply to any passenger on a diverted flight

---

## 5. Liability

Meridian Airlines provides first aid on a reasonable endeavours basis. Meridian does not warrant the availability of specific medical equipment or the medical expertise of volunteer medical professionals onboard. Meridian Airlines is not liable for medical outcomes resulting from in-flight medical events.

Passengers with known medical conditions are strongly encouraged to obtain appropriate medical insurance before travel and to complete the MEDIF process where required (MA-SS-003).

---

## 6. Post-Flight Medical Claims

If a passenger believes that Meridian's handling of a medical situation was inadequate:
1. Submit a formal complaint to feedback@meridianair.com within **30 days** of the incident
2. Include: flight number (e.g., MA-204), date of travel, PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92), and a detailed account of the incident
3. Meridian's safety team will investigate within 20 business days
4. For serious incidents, Meridian may be required to report to aviation safety authorities (IATA, CAA, FAA)
