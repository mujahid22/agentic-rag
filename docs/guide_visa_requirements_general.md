# Meridian Airlines — Visa & Entry Requirements Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-VI-001
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All international passengers on Meridian Airlines flights

---

## 1. Passenger Responsibility

**Meridian Airlines does not process visa applications and is not responsible for verifying a passenger's entry eligibility.** The sole responsibility for holding valid and complete travel documentation rests with the passenger.

Meridian Airlines provides general guidance as a convenience. Requirements change frequently and without notice. Passengers must verify requirements with:
- The destination country's official embassy or consulate
- Official government immigration websites
- IATA Travel Centre (timaticweb.com) — the industry standard database

---

## 2. Consequences of Missing Documentation

If a passenger boards a Meridian Airlines flight without required entry documentation:
- The passenger may be denied entry at the destination and returned to the origin
- Meridian Airlines is obligated under ICAO regulations to return the passenger and may be fined by the destination country
- **The passenger bears the full cost of return carriage**
- No refund is issued on the original ticket; no-show/cancellation penalties apply per MA-CX-001/002

---

## 3. Common Electronic Travel Authorisations (ETAs)

Many countries require electronic pre-authorisation before travel, separate from a visa. Key examples relevant to Meridian routes:

| Destination | ETA/ESTA Type | Who Needs It | Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| United States | ESTA | Visa Waiver Programme nationals | esta.cbp.dhs.gov |
| Canada | eTA | Most non-US foreign nationals | canada.ca/eta |
| United Kingdom | ETA | Non-visa nationals from designated countries | gov.uk/guidance/apply-for-an-electronic-travel-authorisation |
| Australia | ETA or eVisitor | Passport-dependent | immi.homeaffairs.gov.au |
| New Zealand | NZeTA | Most visitors | immigration.govt.nz |
| UAE (Dubai hub) | UAE visa on arrival | Many nationalities eligible | Check icp.gov.ae |

Processing times for ETAs/ESTAs range from 15 minutes to several days. Passengers should apply well in advance of travel.

---

## 4. Visas on Arrival

Some destinations served by Meridian Airlines offer visa-on-arrival (VOA) to eligible nationalities. VOA availability depends on:
- Passenger's passport/nationality
- Duration and purpose of stay
- Travel history and current geopolitical conditions

Meridian Airlines cannot guarantee VOA will be available or granted at any destination. Passengers who intend to rely on VOA should have proof of return travel (Meridian booking confirmation with PNR, e.g., MA-XKPL92) and sufficient funds readily available.

---

## 5. Transit Visa Requirements

A transit visa may be required even if a passenger is not entering the country but merely transiting through the airport:

- **Airside transit (TWOV):** Remaining in the international zone without passing through immigration
- **Landside transit:** Clearing immigration to transit (e.g., to access a different terminal)

Transit visa requirements are highly nationality-specific. Reference MA-VI-002 for detailed transit rules by route.

---

## 6. Checking Requirements Before Travel

Meridian Airlines recommends the following verification timeline:

| Timing Before Travel | Action |
|---|---|
| At time of booking | Check IATA Timatic via meridianair.com/travelinfo |
| 4–6 weeks before | Apply for visa if required |
| 1 week before | Recheck requirements for any recent changes |
| Day before | Confirm all documents are in order and easily accessible |

Meridian Travel Information Tool: meridianair.com/travelinfo (enter nationality, destination, transit point). Results are powered by IATA Timatic and are updated in real time.
