# Meridian Airlines — Weather & Force Majeure Disruption Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-DC-004
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All Meridian Airlines-operated flights (prefix MA-)

---

## 1. Force Majeure Definition

Force majeure events are circumstances beyond Meridian Airlines' reasonable control that make it impossible or unsafe to operate a flight as scheduled. These include:

- **Severe weather:** Hurricanes, blizzards, volcanic ash clouds, dense fog, or lightning conditions preventing safe operation
- **Air Traffic Control (ATC) restrictions:** Government-imposed ground stops, capacity restrictions, or ATC strikes
- **Airport closures:** Runway closures, terminal evacuations, or airport operational failures
- **Security incidents:** Bomb threats, security breaches, or terrorism-related closures
- **Government actions:** Airspace bans, border closures, or military restrictions
- **Natural disasters:** Earthquakes, floods, or other events affecting airport infrastructure

---

## 2. Passenger Rights Under Force Majeure

Under force majeure, Meridian Airlines is **not liable for cash compensation** as defined in MA-DC-001 or MA-DC-003. However, the following passenger rights remain in effect:

### 2.1 Rebooking (Always Applies)
- Meridian will rebook passengers on the next available Meridian flight at no charge
- If no Meridian flight within 12 hours: partner airline rebooking arranged where possible
- If no alternative within 24 hours: full refund must be offered

### 2.2 Care and Assistance (Applies for Delays Over 3 Hours)

| Delay Duration | Entitlement |
|---|---|
| 3–6 hours | Meal voucher USD 20 (MA-MV-XXXXXX) |
| 6–12 hours | Meal voucher USD 40 |
| Over 12 hours | Meal voucher USD 60 + hotel accommodation if required + airport transfer |

Care and assistance entitlements apply during force majeure even though cash compensation does not.

---

## 3. Weather Waiver Policy

When Meridian Airlines proactively identifies severe weather affecting a route, a **Weather Waiver** is issued for affected booking dates. The waiver reference format is: **MA-WW-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-WW-XKP192).

A Weather Waiver allows affected passengers to:
- Rebook to any Meridian flight on the same route within **14 days** of the original travel date at no change fee
- Cancel for a full refund regardless of fare class
- Rebook to a different destination (subject to fare difference) to avoid the weather-affected region

### 3.1 How to Find Your Weather Waiver
- Meridian website: Travel Alerts → Active Waivers → Enter departure date and route
- Email notification sent to booking address if the booking PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92) is in the affected pool
- Contact Centre: +1-800-MERIDIAN

---

## 4. Long-Duration Disruptions (Over 5 Hours)

For disruptions expected to last over 5 hours from the scheduled departure time:
- Meridian will provide a written status update to all affected passengers every 2 hours
- Passengers may cancel their journey at any point and receive a full refund, regardless of fare class and original refundability conditions
- Meridian staff will be stationed at affected departure gates

---

## 5. Passenger Responsibilities

During a weather or force majeure disruption, passengers should:
- Monitor the Meridian app and email for updates (opt-in to push notifications in app settings)
- Do not leave the airport without checking with Meridian ground staff if rebooking is needed
- Keep booking PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92) and e-ticket number (MA-XXXXXXXXXXXXX) accessible for rebooking

Passengers who leave the airport before rebooking is confirmed may lose their place in the rebooking queue.
