# Meridian Airlines — Codeshare Partners Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-CS-001
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All passengers on Meridian Airlines codeshare flights

---

## 1. What is a Codeshare Flight?

A codeshare flight is a flight marketed and sold by Meridian Airlines (with a flight number beginning **MA-**) but operated by a partner airline using their own aircraft and crew. For example, flight **MA-450** may be physically operated by partner airline ArcticAir, with the same aircraft, crew, and service standards as ArcticAir's own flights.

Codeshare flights are identified in booking confirmations and boarding passes with the notation: "Operated by [Partner Airline Name]."

## 3. What Changes on a Codeshare Flight

When you book a Meridian-marketed codeshare flight, the following apply:

| Aspect | Applies |
|---|---|
| Meridian check-in and boarding procedures | **No** — use operating carrier's check-in |
| Meridian Miles earning | **Yes** — miles earned per MA-MM-001 on qualifying fares |
| Meridian baggage policy | **Partial** — Meridian's policy applies if ticket is on a single Meridian PNR |
| Meridian lounge access | **Yes** — if entitled (check lounge at operating airline's terminal) |
| Modifications and cancellations | Through Meridian (Contact Centre) or operating carrier |
| Seat selection | Through Meridian website/app where possible; operating carrier's website as fallback |

---

## 4. Baggage on Codeshare Flights

If your journey is on a **single Meridian PNR** (e.g., MA-XKPL92) and includes codeshare segments:
- The baggage allowance of the **Meridian ticket class** applies for the entire journey
- Bags checked at the first departure point are tagged to the final destination
- Excess baggage fees are charged by the operating carrier at the airport for that segment

If your journey involves **separate PNRs** (one Meridian, one partner):
- Each carrier's own baggage policy applies separately
- Bags must be rechecked at the connection point

---

## 5. Check-in for Codeshare Flights

For a Meridian-marketed codeshare flight:
- Check in via **Meridian's website/app** if the codeshare partner supports it (shown at online check-in)
- Otherwise, check in at the **operating carrier's check-in counter** at the airport
- Always present the **Meridian PNR** (e.g., MA-XKPL92) — this is the master booking reference

---

## 6. Service Standards on Codeshare Flights

Meridian Airlines markets codeshare flights but does not control the service on board. Meals, entertainment, and cabin standards are those of the **operating carrier**. Meridian cannot guarantee the same service levels as on Meridian-operated flights.

Complaints about service on codeshare flights should be directed to the operating carrier in the first instance. If unresolved, escalate to feedback@meridianair.com with the MA- PNR and codeshare flight number.
