# Meridian Airlines — Medical Clearance Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-SS-003
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** Passengers with medical conditions requiring special consideration

---

## 1. When Medical Clearance is Required

Meridian Airlines requires a **Medical Information Form (MEDIF)** for passengers in the following situations:

| Condition | MEDIF Required |
|---|---|
| Recent surgery (within 10 days) | Yes |
| Recent cardiac event (within 6 weeks) | Yes |
| Pregnancy beyond 28 weeks | Yes (see Section 4) |
| Contagious disease | Yes (and may be denied boarding) |
| Stretcher travel | Yes |
| Supplemental oxygen required in-flight | Yes |
| Plaster cast applied within 48 hours | Yes |
| Recent major trauma or fractures | Yes |
| Mental health condition with risk of inflight episode | Yes |

Passengers who are fit to fly but require special seating or assistance (wheelchair, aisle seat) do not require MEDIF — use the Special Assistance service (MA-SS-001).

---

## 2. MEDIF Process

### 2.1 Obtaining the MEDIF Form
The Medical Information Form is available at:
- meridianair.com/medif
- Any Meridian Airport Ticket Office
- Contact: medicalservices@meridianair.com

### 2.2 Completing the MEDIF
The form must be completed by a **licensed medical practitioner** (physician, GP, or specialist). Self-certification is not accepted.

The form covers:
- Diagnosis and current condition
- Fitness to fly statement
- Special requirements in-flight (oxygen, stretcher, medication)
- Medications being carried and in what quantity
- Emergency contact and treating physician details

### 2.3 Submission
- Submit MEDIF to medicalservices@meridianair.com at least **72 hours** before departure
- Reference your booking PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92) in the subject line
- Meridian's medical team reviews and responds within **48 hours**
- Medical Clearance Reference issued upon approval: **MA-MC-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-MC-XKP192)

## 3. In-Flight Medical Equipment

| Equipment | Availability |
|---|---|
| First aid kit | All Meridian flights |
| Automated External Defibrillator (AED) | All Meridian flights |
| Doctor's kit (prescription medications) | All Meridian flights |
| Supplemental oxygen | Available on request with advance MEDIF approval |
| Stretcher | Wide-body aircraft only; advance booking required |

Passengers requiring supplemental oxygen must declare this in the MEDIF. Personal oxygen concentrators (POCs) are accepted if FAA/EASA-approved; list of approved devices at meridianair.com/oxygen.

---

## 4. Pregnancy Travel Policy

| Gestational Stage | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Up to 28 weeks | No MEDIF required; travel as normal |
| 28–36 weeks (single) | MEDIF required; travel permitted with clearance |
| 28–32 weeks (multiple pregnancy) | MEDIF required; travel permitted with clearance |
| Over 36 weeks (single) | Travel not accepted |
| Over 32 weeks (multiple pregnancy) | Travel not accepted |

A letter from the attending physician confirming gestational age and fitness to fly is required for travel between 28–36 weeks. The letter must be dated within **7 days** of departure.

---

## 5. Medication in Cabin

Passengers may carry prescribed medications in the cabin:
- Must be in original pharmaceutical packaging with pharmacy label showing name and prescription details
- Liquid medications over 100 ml require a doctor's letter and must be declared at security
- Controlled substances (opioids, benzodiazepines) require additional documentation per destination country regulations

Meridian cabin crew can store insulin and temperature-sensitive medications in onboard refrigeration on request.

---

## 6. Onboard Medical Emergency

In the event of a medical emergency in-flight:
- Cabin crew will assess and provide first aid
- A physician onboard may be requested via PA announcement
- Medical radio consultation available with ground-based physicians via the flight deck
- In serious cases, the captain may divert the aircraft to the nearest suitable airport
- Diversion decisions are made solely by the captain in consultation with medical authorities

Meridian Airlines accepts no liability for medical outcomes resulting from conditions that were not disclosed before travel.
