# Meridian Airlines — Travel Health Requirements Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-VI-003
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All passengers on Meridian Airlines international flights

---

## 1. Overview

Some countries require proof of specific vaccinations or health documentation as a condition of entry. Meridian Airlines checks health documentation requirements at the time of booking and notifies passengers in the confirmation email.

**Passengers are responsible for obtaining and carrying all required health documents.** Meridian provides this guide for convenience only; requirements may change without notice.

---

## 2. Yellow Fever Vaccination

### 2.1 Who Must Carry a Yellow Fever Certificate
A Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate (YVFC), also known as the "Yellow Card" or Carte Jaune, is required by certain countries in:
- Sub-Saharan Africa (e.g., Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, Uganda, Cameroon)
- South America (e.g., Brazil, Colombia, Peru, Ecuador)

Requirements apply in two scenarios:
1. **Entry requirement:** The destination country requires proof of yellow fever vaccination for all arriving passengers
2. **Transit country:** The country requires proof if the passenger has transited through a yellow fever-endemic country in the past 6 days

### 2.2 Certificate Format
- Must be issued by an authorised vaccination centre
- Must include: passenger name, date of birth, vaccine name and batch number, date of vaccination, vaccinating clinician's signature and stamp
- Valid for **life** (per WHO regulations since 2016 — the former 10-year limit no longer applies)

### 2.3 Failure to Present
Passengers unable to present a required YVFC may be vaccinated at the airport (if a vaccination centre is available) or denied entry. Meridian Airlines may deny boarding to passengers without required documentation to avoid fines and return-flight obligations.

## 3. Other Required Vaccinations (Destination-Specific)

| Destination Region | Possible Requirements |
|---|---|
| Saudi Arabia (Hajj / Umrah season) | Meningococcal meningitis vaccine certificate required |
| India | Cholera vaccination recommended (not mandatory); yellow fever if transiting endemic country |
| Sub-Saharan Africa (general) | Yellow fever (see above); typhoid and malaria prophylaxis recommended |
| South America | Yellow fever (some countries mandatory); typhoid recommended |

Recommended vaccinations are not checked by Meridian but are strongly advised by the WHO and health authorities. Passengers should consult a travel health clinic at least **4–6 weeks before travel**.

---

## 4. Health Declarations

Some countries periodically require incoming passengers to complete a health declaration form. Forms may be:
- Paper (collected onboard or at immigration)
- Digital (submitted in advance via the destination country's passenger arrival system)

When health declarations are required, Meridian Airlines will:
- Notify passengers at time of booking
- Include declaration form link in the check-in reminder email
- Distribute paper forms during the flight if applicable

---

## 5. Fitness to Fly

Passengers with communicable diseases or symptoms including fever over 38°C, persistent cough, vomiting, or diarrhoea may be denied boarding if considered a health risk to other passengers. Meridian Airlines follows IATA and WHO health risk protocols.

Passengers who are unsure about their fitness to fly should consult a physician before travel. See MA-SS-003 for medical clearance requirements.
