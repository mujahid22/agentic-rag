# Meridian Airlines — Denied Boarding (Overbooking) Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-DC-003
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All Meridian Airlines-operated flights (prefix MA-)

---

## 1. What is Denied Boarding?

Denied boarding occurs when a passenger holds a confirmed booking and valid boarding pass but is unable to board due to the flight being oversold. This is distinct from denial of boarding due to the passenger's own fault (e.g., missing documents, disruptive behaviour).

---

## 2. Voluntary Denied Boarding (Bumping)

Meridian Airlines first seeks **volunteers** at the gate when a flight is oversold. Volunteers who agree to travel on a later flight are offered:

| Delay on Alternative Flight | Compensation Package |
|---|---|
| Under 2 hours | USD 200 Meridian travel credit (MA-TC-XXXXXX) |
| 2–4 hours | USD 350 Meridian travel credit |
| 4–6 hours | USD 500 Meridian travel credit + meal voucher (USD 30) |
| Over 6 hours | USD 700 Meridian travel credit + meal voucher + hotel if overnight |

Volunteers also receive:
- Confirmed seat on the next available Meridian flight at no charge
- Retention of their original booking status and miles earning on the new flight

---

## 3. Involuntary Denied Boarding

If insufficient volunteers come forward, Meridian Airlines may involuntarily deny boarding. Passengers will be selected in the following **last to be involuntarily denied** order (i.e., these passengers are protected first):

1. Unaccompanied minors
2. Passengers with disabilities or medical needs
3. Meridian Miles Platinum members
4. Meridian Miles Gold members
5. First and Business class passengers
6. Passengers connecting to another Meridian flight on the same PNR
7. Passengers with the highest fare paid (most recent booking date as tiebreaker)

---

## 4. Involuntary Denied Boarding Compensation

Passengers involuntarily denied boarding are entitled to:

| Delay to Alternative Arrival | Domestic | International |
|---|---|---|
| Under 2 hours | USD 300 cash or travel credit | USD 400 cash or travel credit |
| 2–4 hours | USD 500 cash or travel credit | USD 700 cash or travel credit |
| Over 4 hours | USD 700 cash or travel credit | USD 1,000 cash or travel credit |

In addition:
- **Full refund option:** If the passenger chooses not to travel, a full refund is provided regardless of fare class
- **Rebooking:** Next available Meridian flight at no charge
- **Meals and accommodation:** Provided if the alternative flight departs the same day (meals) or next day (hotel + meals)

Compensation is paid at the gate by the Meridian gate agent. Cash or credit choice is the passenger's.

---

## 5. Documentation at the Gate

Meridian ground staff will provide the involuntarily denied boarding passenger with:
- Written notice of their rights
- Denied Boarding Certificate (format: **MA-DBR-XXXXXX**, e.g., MA-DBR-XKP192)
- Compensation voucher or cash
- Confirmed alternative booking reference

The Denied Boarding Certificate is required to claim any future benefits related to the incident.

---

## 6. Exclusions

Denied boarding compensation does **not** apply when:
- Boarding is denied due to the passenger arriving after the check-in or gate deadline
- Boarding is denied due to health, safety, or security reasons attributable to the passenger
- The passenger does not hold required travel documents
- The alternative flight arrives within 1 hour of the original scheduled arrival (domestic) or 2 hours (international)
