# Meridian Airlines — Waitlist & Standby Travel Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-FK-004
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All fare classes, all routes

---

## 1. Flight Waitlist

### 1.1 Joining the Waitlist
When a Meridian Airlines flight is sold out, passengers may join the waitlist for that flight. The waitlist is available for booking on Economy Flex, Business, and First class fares only. Economy Saver fares are **not eligible** for the waitlist.

### 1.2 Waitlist Priority
Passengers on the waitlist are prioritised as follows:
1. Meridian Miles Platinum members
2. Meridian Miles Gold members
3. Meridian Miles Silver members
4. Economy Flex, Business, and First class passengers (by booking date — earliest first)

### 1.3 Waitlist Reference
When placed on the waitlist, a Waitlist Reference Number is issued in the format: **MA-WL-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-WL-XKP192). This reference is separate from the PNR.

### 1.4 Confirmation
- If a seat becomes available, the passenger is automatically confirmed and notified via email and SMS.
- Confirmation is typically received within 48 hours of departure for popular routes.
- If no seat becomes available by 2 hours before departure, the waitlist booking is cancelled and a full refund is issued.

## 3. Standby Travel

### 3.1 Definition
Standby travel allows a confirmed passenger to travel on an earlier or later flight than the one booked, subject to seat availability at the time of check-in.

### 3.2 Eligibility

| Fare Class | Standby Eligible | Fee |
|---|---|---|
| Economy Saver | No | — |
| Economy Flex | Yes | USD 50 per sector |
| Business | Yes | USD 0 (free) |
| First | Yes | USD 0 (free) |

### 3.3 How to Request Standby
- At the **airport check-in counter** on the day of travel: inform the agent of the preferred flight
- Standby passengers are boarded after all confirmed passengers
- Standby reference format: **MA-SBY-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-SBY-XKP192)
- If standby is unsuccessful, the passenger must travel on their original confirmed flight

### 3.4 Same-Day Standby
Same-day standby (earlier or later flight on the same calendar day) is available at the airport only. It cannot be requested in advance online.

## 4. Oversold Flights — Volunteer and Involuntary Offloading

### 4.1 Volunteer Bump
When a Meridian Airlines flight is oversold, Meridian will first seek **volunteers** to accept a later flight in exchange for compensation:

| Delay Accepted | Compensation Offered |
|---|---|
| Up to 3 hours | USD 200 Meridian travel credit (MA-TC-XXXXXX) |
| 3–6 hours | USD 400 Meridian travel credit |
| Over 6 hours | USD 600 Meridian travel credit + meal voucher |

Volunteers also retain their original ticket for travel on the later flight.

### 4.2 Involuntary Offloading
If insufficient volunteers come forward, Meridian Airlines may involuntarily offload passengers. Priority for offloading (last to be offloaded):
1. Unaccompanied minors
2. Passengers with medical needs
3. Platinum and Gold status members
4. Business and First class passengers

Passengers involuntarily offloaded are entitled to denied boarding compensation per MA-DC-003.
