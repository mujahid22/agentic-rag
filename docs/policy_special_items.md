# Meridian Airlines — Special Items & Sports Equipment Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-BG-004
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All fare classes, all routes

---

## 1. Sports Equipment

Sports equipment is accepted as checked baggage subject to the fees and conditions below. Each item counts as one piece toward your checked baggage allowance or is charged as an extra bag.

| Sport Item | Max Weight | Max Dimensions | Fee (Domestic) | Fee (International) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bicycle (in hard case) | 30 kg | 292 cm combined | USD 75 | USD 100 |
| Golf bag + clubs | 25 kg | 292 cm combined | USD 50 | USD 75 |
| Surfboard / bodyboard | 25 kg | 292 cm combined | USD 75 | USD 100 |
| Skis / snowboard (in bag) | 25 kg | 292 cm combined | USD 50 | USD 75 |
| Kayak / canoe | Not accepted as baggage — cargo only | — | — | — |
| Dive equipment (tanks empty) | 25 kg | 158 cm combined | Standard bag fee | Standard bag fee |

Advance notice is required for bicycles and surfboards: notify Meridian at least **48 hours before departure** by calling +1-800-MERIDIAN or emailing specialbaggage@meridianair.com.

## 2. Musical Instruments

### 2.1 Small Instruments in Cabin
Instruments that fit within cabin bag dimensions (56 × 36 × 23 cm, max 7 kg) may be carried in the overhead bin and count as the cabin baggage allowance.

### 2.2 Large Instruments — Seat Purchase
Cellos, large guitars, and similar instruments that exceed cabin bag dimensions but fit within a passenger seat may be transported in the cabin by **purchasing an adjacent seat**. The instrument must be secured in a hard case and fastened with the seat belt. Seat purchase at standard Economy fare; instrument travels at no additional baggage fee.

### 2.3 Large Instruments — Checked Hold
Instruments too large for the cabin and not requiring a purchased seat are transported in the hold:

| Instrument Size | Fee (Domestic) | Fee (International) |
|---|---|---|
| Fits within 158 cm combined | Standard checked bag fee | Standard checked bag fee |
| 158–292 cm combined | USD 75 | USD 100 |

Meridian Airlines accepts no liability for damage to instruments transported in the hold beyond standard baggage liability (USD 1,700).

## 3. Mobility Aids and Wheelchairs

Manual and electric wheelchairs are transported **free of charge** on all Meridian Airlines flights, in addition to the standard baggage allowance.

- Manual wheelchairs: accepted in hold or cabin (folding wheelchairs may be stored in overhead bin or closet)
- Electric wheelchairs (lithium battery): battery must be disconnected; maximum battery capacity 300 Wh. Advance notification required at least **48 hours before departure**.
- Mobility scooters: accepted in hold only, same battery requirements as electric wheelchairs

Contact: specialassistance@meridianair.com or +1-800-MERIDIAN option 3.

---

## 4. Prams and Pushchairs

One pram or pushchair per infant ticket is accepted **free of charge** in the hold, in addition to the standard baggage allowance. Prams may also be gate-checked and collected at the aircraft door at the destination.

---

## 5. Pets in the Hold (Checked Cargo)

Pets travelling in the pressurised cargo hold must be booked through Meridian Cargo, not as standard checked baggage.
- Advance booking: minimum **72 hours** before departure
- Pet booking reference format: **MA-PET-XXXXXXX**
- Approved IATA-compliant container required
- Temperature restrictions apply — some routes and seasons have embargo periods

Contact: pettravel@meridianair.com for route-specific availability and fees.

---

## 6. Prohibited Items in Checked Baggage

The following must **never** be placed in checked baggage:
- Lithium batteries (spare/loose) — must travel in carry-on
- Electronic cigarettes and vaping devices — must travel in carry-on
- Flammable liquids (perfume over 500 ml, nail varnish remover over 500 ml)
- Cash, jewellery, passports, or irreplaceable items (Meridian accepts no liability)
- Firearms and ammunition without prior Dangerous Goods approval (MA-HS-001)
