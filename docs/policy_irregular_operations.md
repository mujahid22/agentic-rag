# Meridian Airlines — Irregular Operations Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-DC-002
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All Meridian Airlines-operated flights (prefix MA-)

---

## 1. Definition of Irregular Operation (IRROP)

An irregular operation occurs when a Meridian Airlines flight is:
- **Cancelled** by Meridian Airlines before or after boarding
- **Diverted** to an unplanned airport
- **Significantly delayed** (3+ hours at destination — see MA-DC-001)
- **Operated with aircraft downgrade** (smaller aircraft causing seat removal)

This policy covers airline-initiated disruptions only. Passenger-initiated cancellations and no-shows are governed by MA-CX-001 through MA-CX-004.

---

## 2. Flight Cancellation — Passenger Rights

If Meridian Airlines cancels a flight, passengers are entitled to **one of the following** (passenger's choice):

### Option A — Full Refund
- Full refund of the ticket to the original payment method
- Processed within **72 hours** — no passenger action required
- Applies to all fare classes including Economy Saver
- Refund reference format: **MA-REF-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-REF-XKP192)

### Option B — Rebooking at No Charge
- Rebooked to the next available Meridian flight on the same route
- If no Meridian flight within 8 hours: rebooked on a partner airline at Meridian's expense
- If downgraded to a lower cabin: fare difference refunded within 7 business days
- No rebooking fee applies regardless of original fare class

### Option C — Meridian Travel Credit
- Travel credit (MA-TC-XXXXXX) for 120% of the original ticket value
- Valid for 24 months
- Applicable to any Meridian flight

---

## 3. Notification Requirements

Meridian Airlines will notify affected passengers of a cancellation via:
- Email to the address on the booking (MA-XKPL92 confirmation email address)
- SMS to the mobile number on the booking
- Push notification via the Meridian app
- Airport display boards and PA announcements

Notification timeline:
- If cancellation is known more than **14 days** before departure: full notification issued
- If cancellation is known **2–14 days** before departure: alternative flight offered simultaneously
- If cancellation occurs on the day of travel: Meridian service desk at the airport handles rebooking

---

## 4. Diversion

If a Meridian flight is diverted to an unplanned airport:
- Ground transport provided to the original destination at Meridian's expense
- If ground transport is not feasible, a connecting flight is arranged
- Meal and refreshment vouchers provided during the wait
- Hotel accommodation provided if diversion results in overnight stay

---

## 5. Aircraft Downgrade (Seat Removal)

If an aircraft substitution results in fewer seats in a cabin:

| Affected Passenger | Compensation |
|---|---|
| Economy Saver | USD 50 service credit + apology letter |
| Economy Flex | USD 100 service credit |
| Business (downgraded to Economy) | Full fare difference refunded in cash + USD 150 inconvenience credit |
| First (downgraded to Business) | Full fare difference refunded in cash + USD 200 inconvenience credit |

Meridian will attempt to minimise downgrades by offering voluntary cabin downgrades with compensation before applying involuntary downgrades.

---

## 6. Extraordinary Circumstances (Force Majeure)

The following circumstances are classified as extraordinary and do not entitle passengers to cash compensation (Options A and B — refund/rebooking — still apply):

- Severe weather conditions that make flight unsafe
- Air traffic control strikes or restrictions
- Security incidents at the airport
- Government-imposed travel bans or airspace closures
- Civil unrest or acts of terrorism

Meal vouchers and hotel accommodation are still provided during extraordinary circumstance delays exceeding 4 hours, even without cash compensation entitlement.
