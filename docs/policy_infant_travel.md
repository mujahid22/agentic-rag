# Meridian Airlines — Infant Travel Policy
**Policy Reference:** MA-FA-001
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** Infants under 2 years of age

---

## 1. Infant Age Definition

An **infant** on Meridian Airlines is a child **under 2 years of age** on the date of travel (outbound departure date for return journeys).

If a child turns 2 during the trip, they must hold a child ticket for the return journey.

---

## 2. Lap Infant (Infant on Adult's Lap)

### 2.1 Fare
Lap infants travel at **10% of the applicable adult fare** (Economy, Business, or First) on international routes. On domestic routes, the lap infant fare is a **flat USD 25 per sector**.

### 2.2 Restrictions
- Maximum **1 lap infant per accompanying adult**
- Lap infants do not receive a seat; they sit on the adult's lap
- Lap infants are not permitted in exit rows
- Maximum **2 lap infants per row** (safety regulation)
- Infants must be held during takeoff, landing, and turbulence (no separate seatbelt; adult's belt around both is not permitted — infant uses the provided loop belt)

### 2.3 Loop Belt
A loop seatbelt extension for lap infants is provided by cabin crew on request. One loop belt per infant.

---

## 3. Infant with Seat

Passengers may purchase a full fare seat for an infant:
- Any fare class available
- Infant must travel in an approved child safety seat (CRS) secured to the aircraft seat
- CRS must be FAA/EASA-approved; approval label must be visible on the seat
- CRS installation: airline allows use in all seats except exit rows

Benefits of purchasing a seat for an infant:
- Safer for the child (especially on turbulent or long flights)
- Infant can sleep in CRS more comfortably
- No need to hold infant during the entire flight

---

## 4. Bassinet (Infant Cot)

A bassinet (sky cot) is a small pull-down cot fixed to the aircraft bulkhead wall, available for infants up to **approximately 9 kg and 76 cm**.

### 4.1 Availability
- Available on wide-body international aircraft only (not narrow-body domestic)
- Limited number per aircraft (typically 4–8 bassinets)

### 4.2 How to Request
- Request at time of booking: Contact Centre +1-800-MERIDIAN or specialassistance@meridianair.com
- Reference your PNR (e.g., MA-XKPL92)
- Bassinet request reference: **MA-BS-XXXXXX** (e.g., MA-BS-XKP192)
- Confirmed on a first-come, first-served basis — **not guaranteed**
- Bulkhead seat assigned with bassinet request; extra legroom but no under-seat storage at bulkhead

### 4.3 Restrictions
- Bassinet not usable during takeoff, landing, or turbulence — infant must be held
- Bassinet is fixed to the wall and cannot be repositioned

---

## 5. Infant Baggage Allowance

| Ticket Type | Baggage Allowance |
|---|---|
| Lap infant (all routes) | 1 checked bag up to 10 kg + 1 pram/pushchair (free) |
| Infant with seat | Same allowance as accompanying adult's fare class |

Breast milk, formula, and baby food: no quantity restriction in carry-on; declare at security. See MA-BG-001.

---

## 6. Infant Travel Documents

Infants require a valid passport for international travel. Many countries do not allow infants on a parent's passport. Verify requirements for the specific destination at meridianair.com/travelinfo.

Domestic travel: national ID or birth certificate accepted as infant identification in most jurisdictions.
