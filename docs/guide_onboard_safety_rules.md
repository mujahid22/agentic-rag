# Meridian Airlines — Onboard Safety Rules Guide
**Policy Reference:** MA-HS-001
**Effective Date:** 1 November 2025
**Applies To:** All passengers on all Meridian Airlines flights

---

## 1. Seatbelt Policy

- Seatbelts must be fastened **at all times when seated**, even when the seatbelt sign is off
- Seatbelts must be fastened during: taxi, takeoff, landing, turbulence, and whenever the seatbelt sign is illuminated
- The captain may illuminate the seatbelt sign at any time — compliance is mandatory and enforceable
- Passengers who refuse to comply with seatbelt instructions may be subject to removal from the flight and applicable fines under aviation law

---

## 2. Electronic Devices — PED (Portable Electronic Devices)

### 2.1 Permitted Devices
All personal electronic devices (phones, tablets, laptops, e-readers) are permitted throughout the flight when in **Flight Mode (Airplane Mode)**, including during takeoff and landing.

### 2.2 Mobile Phones and Calls
- Voice calls and mobile data (cellular connection) are **not permitted** during the flight
- Voice over Wi-Fi (VoIP calls via MeridianConnect Wi-Fi) are permitted but discouraged in the cabin out of respect for other passengers
- Calls in the cabin are discouraged; designated areas near lavatory bulkheads may be used

### 2.3 Devices with Cellular Restrictions
- All devices must be in Airplane Mode from aircraft door close to aircraft door open at destination
- Bluetooth: permitted for connecting to headphones, keyboards, and other personal devices

### 2.4 Large Electronic Devices
Laptops and tablets may be used during the entire flight including taxi. They must be stowed in the seat pocket or under the seat for takeoff, landing, and turbulence.

---

## 3. Prohibited Items Onboard

In addition to items prohibited in checked baggage (MA-BG-004), the following are prohibited in the cabin:

- **Smoking:** strictly prohibited on all Meridian Airlines flights, including in lavatories. The lavatory smoke detector is a safety device — tampering with it is a federal offence in many jurisdictions. Penalty: USD 2,000–5,000 fine and possible arrest on landing.
- **Electronic cigarettes and vaping:** prohibited for use in-flight (devices may be carried in cabin but not used)
- **Drones:** not permitted in the cabin or in checked baggage without prior cargo approval
- **Hoverboards / personal electric transporters:** not permitted due to lithium battery fire risk

---

## 4. Oxygen and Pressurisation

All Meridian cabin aircraft maintain cabin pressure equivalent to an altitude of **6,000–8,000 feet**. This is a normal and safe environment for most passengers. Passengers with certain medical conditions should consult a physician before flying (see MA-SS-003).

In the event of rapid decompression, oxygen masks will drop automatically. Passengers must:
- Pull the mask towards you to activate oxygen flow
- Place over nose and mouth
- Breathe normally
- Secure your own mask before assisting others

---

## 5. Safety Briefing

A safety briefing is conducted before every flight by cabin crew. Passengers must:
- Pay attention to the safety briefing
- Read the safety card in the seat pocket
- Identify the nearest emergency exit (it may be behind you)

Passengers who have listened to the safety briefing on a previous flight are still required to pay attention — aircraft configurations differ between aircraft types.

---

## 6. Laser Pointers and Illuminated Devices

Directing a laser pointer or any high-intensity light at any aircraft, including Meridian Airlines aircraft, is illegal in most jurisdictions and may result in arrest and prosecution. Laser pointers are prohibited on Meridian Airlines flights.

---

## 7. Compliance and Authority of Crew

Meridian cabin crew have the authority to issue lawful instructions to all passengers for safety and security reasons. Non-compliance is a criminal offence in most aviation jurisdictions. Passengers who refuse to comply may be:
- Diverted to an alternate airport
- Met by law enforcement on arrival
- Placed on the Meridian no-fly list
- Subject to prosecution
